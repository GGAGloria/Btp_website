Go2Africa Project

Go2Africa is one of the founding projects of Beyond The Pivot (BTP) of the University of Hong Kong. Go2Africa has been in operation for over ten years and is the only overseas project of the BTP. We consider our project exceptional. Our activities organized are exceptionally rich. The visit is exceptionally challenging. Guaranteed is the exceptional amount of learning from such experience.

It all began with a humble curiosity of our pioneers – a curiosity to learn about the African culture. Curiosity drives our visit. Our visit improves our understanding of the life of people living in Africa. When we understand and befriend people in Africa, we start to care about them. At the end, we cannot help vowing to contribute to the improvement of lives of poor people in Africa.

We strongly believe, anyone who is willing to take the first step with us to Africa will start to appreciate how blessed we are – with all the modernization and materials available to us in the developed world. We will come to realize that countless people still live in very poor conditions and they would appreciate a little help from us.

Go2Africa needs you! The noble goal of helping Africans can never be achieved by any single man or woman. No, we would not expect anyone to devote the rest of lives helping Africans after one visit. It is our hope that through seeing Africa up close and personal, we will develop awareness of the poverty in Africa and many parts of the world. Like seeds, such awareness is often buried deep in our heart but one day it will germinate and translate into actions of helping the needy around us – not just the Africans.

In these ten years, our understanding of Africa has improved and our interest has evolved. Naturally, the mix of activities and service is adjusted according to the local needs, our interest and ability. Nonetheless, the project in recent years has almost always included teaching in primary and secondary schools, building public toilets, supporting and assisting hepatitis B screening, and disseminating health knowledge. Through these activities, we contribute to improve local health conditions, to raise the educational standards of young people and to broaden their horizons, and therefore to improve their well-being in the longer term. To the participants, such activities help raise our awareness of poverty situation in Africa.

1. Volunteer teaching activities: Volunteers will substitute local teachers in teaching the regular classes, and thus get close contact with local students and teachers, and understand the difficulties of teaching and learning. Before the trip, volunteers will be briefed the local teaching and learning environment. They will learn how to prepare lessons and teach in such environment.

2. Donate and build latrines: From scratch, volunteers will design the toilet, purchase materials, recruit workers, supervise and participate in the construction work to its completion.

3. Donation for hepatitis B screening: In cooperation with the local government and charities in Ghana, volunteers will help perform hepatitis B screening for local villagers, and disseminate related health information.

The visit and the activities serve as a bridge to our understanding of Africa. As always, how much we gain from the visit depends on our learning attitude and how much effort we put in. If you share our values, we would be happy to welcome you aboard.
