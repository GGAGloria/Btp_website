Home Kong Project

Hong Kong has always been described by words like ‘vibrant’, ‘financial center’, ‘soaring skyscraper’, etc. However, on the other side of prosperous image, Hong Kong is also a region with great disparity between rich and poor where the disadvantaged people living in the harsh reality.

BTP Home Kong intends to dive into the other side of the prosperous city Hong Kong and aims to bring warmth and give care to the less advantaged groups in Hong Kong. BTP Home Kong consists of three project teams, Global Youth Connect (‘GYC’), One Heart (OH!), and Catering Care.

Global Youth Connect (GYC)

Cooperating with Youth Global Network, a Hong Kong based NGO, GYC promotes communication between asylum seekers in Hong Kong and volunteers so as to overcome the culture barrier. There is no giver or receiver in this project. We are mutual friends exploring Hong Kong and supporting the community together.

GYC organizes various indoor and outdoor activities twice a month, including Inner Challenge (hiking), Kam Tin Cultural Trip, picking up liters on Hung Shing Ye Beach in Lamma Island, equal sharing in Sham Shui Po, Postcard Exchange on Christmas Day and etc. Volunteers get the precious chance to have a personal experience about how asylum seekers come to Hong Kong, living conditions of asylum seekers in Hong Kong, and how they feel about their life in Hong Kong. We hope the asylum seekers may better blend into the local society and in turn the mainstream society may share more spotlight to asylum seekers.

One Heart (OH!)

Staring from 2016, OH! Project, in collaboration with ISS Hope Center, aims to support the ethnic minority teenagers through mentor-mentee program. Pairing ethnic minority teenagers from local secondary school with students from the University of Hong Kong, mentor and mentee could share their lives, their achievements and troubles in life, their cultures and dreams through letter exchange, career workshop and outings in Hong Kong. Through the mentorship program, mentors and mentees could build sincere and long-term relationships and experience the inclusiveness and diversity of Hong Kong culture.

Catering Care

Catering Care is a new project in face of the corona-virus pandemic. People spend less time eating out because of the social distancing and lock-down policies. Consequentially, the medium or small-scale restaurants, in particular, has been going through a hard time to survive in this challenging time. Gone with the restaurants are the laughter and memories of enjoying food with families and friends.

Moreover, the food insecurity group has been expanding because of the soaring unemployment rate. Our project aims to create a win-win situation where both such catering businesses and the food insecurity groups are supported by encouraging people to donate money for purchase of charity meals. We devote to strengthen the connection within the community and build up the reliance through such food partnership.
